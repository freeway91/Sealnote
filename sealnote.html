⚠️ Important: Your notes are stored locally

Sealnote stores your encrypted notes inside your browser. This keeps them private, but it also means:

  • Clearing browser data will permanently delete your notes
  • Moving this file to a new folder may lose access to notes
  • Switching browsers or devices means starting fresh

✅ Solution: Export a backup regularly from the ⚙ menu → "Export all notes"

🛡 Security guide

✅ What Sealnote protects Your notes are encrypted with AES-256-GCM. Even if someone copies your browser data or backup file, they cannot read your notes without your password.
⚠ What Sealnote cannot protect If your device is compromised, an attacker can read your notes while you have them open — because the decryption happens on your device. No notes app in the world can protect against a compromised device.

To stay safe, follow these habits:

🔑 Use a strong, unique password — avoid common words, names, or dates. Use a password manager.
🚫 Don't open unknown files or links — malware on your device can capture your password the moment you type it.
🔄 Keep your OS and browser updated — updates patch security vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.
📦 Export a backup regularly — your notes exist only on this device. A backup protects against data loss.
📶 Avoid public Wi-Fi — Sealnote never sends data online, but other apps on your device might expose you.
🔒 Log out when done — especially on shared computers. Your session auto-expires after 15 minutes of inactivity.
🔍 Sealnote is fully open source. Every line of encryption code is auditable. Your trust should be based on code you can verify, not promises.
